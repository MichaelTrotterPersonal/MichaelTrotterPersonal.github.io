Pilot

Recreational Aviation

In 2001 Mick attained his Solo Glider Pilots Licence at the Gliding Club of Victoria in Benalla.

Between 2003 and 2005 he completed over 110 skydives and obtained a Skydiver 'C' rating.

In 2011 he took up paragliding and accrued more than 120 hours of flight, becoming particularly proficient at low-level coastal soaring.

In 2021 he completed an RAAus Recreational Pilots Certificate in an Aeroprakt A-22 Foxbat flying with Merit Aviation in Moruya, NSW.

In early 2022 he then converted to a Recreational Pilots License with Controlled Airspace/Aerodrome Endorsement in Piper PA-28 Warriors and Archers flying out of Canberra Airport.

Professional Aviation

Mick obtained an RPAS Controllers Certificate with Aeronautical Radio Operators Certificate in 2015 before going on to fly dozens of succesful aerial surveys and countless engineering test flights.

He holds RePL ratings for both aeroplane and multirotor types.

From 2007 to 2010 he worked at Mt Hotham in the Victorian snowfields. While in the Lift Operations department he took on the role of Helipad Manager, supervising the Hotham ground operations for the Falls Creek to Mt Hotham helilink.

Mick's latest flights

Mick is an active, member of the Canberra Aeroclub. He tries to fly at least once a week in the club's Piper Archer VH-MIZ.

He can often be seen arriving to work unusually late, or else unusually early, in an attempt to catch the best flying conditions for the day.
