3.6 Something Original

Runway crosswind and daylight hours calculator

Notes: I've implemented this runway crosswind calculator in JavaScript. It takes:

  • One or multiple runway numbers (01 to 36, representing the bearing of the runway rounded to the nearest 10 degrees)
  • A wind direction (in degrees)
  • A wind speed (any unit)
It will plot the wind and runways, then calculate the best runway (least crosswind) and display the headwind and crosswind components of the wind.
It will also request the user's location, which it sends to a sunset/sunrise API to determine the local times for daylight flying.
NOTE: I've discovered that the location functionality probably doesn't work on partch so you may not get the prompt and may only see a 'location needed' message. However, the bulk of the functionality is fine.

It should hopefully be simple enough for anyone to use, though it does assume some aviation knowledge in the format of runway numbers.

You can also see a screen capture and brief description of the tool here: computing.html#xwind.
